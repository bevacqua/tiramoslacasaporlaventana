When is it?

After over three years together, we're getting married!

The wedding party is on april 16th, at day time — from 12:30 pm and until 09:00 pm. There's going to be a cocktail, followed by dancing, barbecue, more dancing, drinks, desserts, cotillon, and more.

If you have kids, babies, or people you've otherwise created, you can keep calm and bring them. We have planned plenty of activities so that they have fun as well!

There's lots of room to park your car or helicopter, so don't worry about that!

We recommend 20 hours of fasting, a couple of dance lessons, and an urge to have a good time!

Where is it?

At the Álamos de Cañuelas ranch, a gorgeous countryside field packed with fresh air, chickens, goatlings, and even peacocks! Alas, we won't be hunting for food... we'll be enjoying delicious argentine barbecue, but the goatlings and peacocks roaming around the ranch won't be included!

The reception cocktail is right outside the lounge. For lunch, we'll head into the lounge, so that we don't fall prey to global warming.

The drink bar, tea service, candy buffet, chickens, and fresh air will all be found outside the lounge.

Álamos is on national route 3 — RN3. For directions click here.

We hope you like Álamos as much as we do!

What should I wear?

Our dress code is casual elegant.

When picking your dress, keep in mind that — weather permitting — the party will take place both indoors and outdoors.

To our fancy women: we suggest that you bring a purse with an extra pair of shoes!

Kids are exempt from our dress code, and they can dress as they please, so don't feel compelled to dress them up if they don't want to!

Keep in mind you'll dance and eat to your heart's content — it'll be essential to dress comfortably!

How to get there?

Here are the GPS coordinates for the ranch.

Latitude34° 56' 5.12" S
Longitude58° 43' 9.35" W

If you don't own a GPS, you like cartography, or you prefer to read instructions and draw your own maps, here's a description of how to arrive at the ranch.

  1. Take Ezeiza—Cañuelas highway, and upon reaching Cañuelas — that is, the end of the highway — head out on the first roundabout where there's an ESSO gas station
  2. Take the first entrance to the right (never to the left), and about 200 m later you'll find another roundabout with an OIL gas station
  3. Make a right, and take the second entrance of the roundabout to the right (never to the left)
  4. There's a big green white sign that clearly spells out Buenos Aires and Ruta 3. Take that highway. There's also another big green sign that says González Catán, San Justo, and Buenos Aires. It's exactly 10.5 km until the Álamos ranch
  5. Immediately, you'll find a very big UTA union field to the right
  6. When you reach km 52, you'll see a huge Jehová's Witnessestemple, and — 200 m later — a walking bridge over the route
  7. On the right-hand side you'll notice a sign that says Petión/Visir (do not take it), continue for 500 m on route 3
  8. To the right you'll find the entrance to Álamos de Cañuelas (you'll see a big wooden sign with the ranch's name and logo)
  9. Take a right and 200 m later you'll find the doorway to the ranch
